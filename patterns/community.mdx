These patterns help you tap into the Canton community effectively — finding the right experts, surfacing mailing list discussions, and identifying potential collaborators.

## Find someone who already solved this

<Note>**Problem.** The right Canton-side expert for "topology" or "wallet kernel" isn't obvious from a forum username alone.</Note>

**Solution.** `find_expert(topic)` ranks authors by topic-relevant contribution volume and total community activity. Top contributors in a given area frequently include the engineers who maintain the relevant component.

**References:**
- [Forum & Community tool](/tools/forum-community)

---

## Search Canton mailing lists, not just the forum

<Note>**Problem.** Foundation governance and many SV ops discussions never make it to the forum — they live on the Canton Network mailing lists.</Note>

**Solution.** `search_mailing_list(query, group?)` covers cip-discuss, cip-vote, cip-announce, grants-discuss, validator-announce, and globalSyncForum. Pass `group="cip-vote"` to drill into formal CIP votes specifically.

**References:**
- [Talks, Docs & Mail tool](/tools/talks-docs-mail)

---

## Find adjacent projects to partner with

<Note>**Problem.** Builders working on adjacent capabilities — for example, a wallet, a DEX, and a bridge — rarely discover each other organically.</Note>

**Solution.** `find_collaboration_opportunities(project)` returns ecosystem projects with overlapping tags and category classifications. Run it once at proposal-time to find potential partners, and again at design-time to align on shared interfaces.
